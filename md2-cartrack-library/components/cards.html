Cards

A card groups related content and actions about a single subject — a vehicle summary, a KPI, an alert. Cards make a dashboard scannable by giving each thing its own contained surface.

Category: SurfacesDesktopImport: Card, CardHeader, CardContent
Use a card to collect everything about one entity. If you're listing many comparable records, a data table is usually clearer than a wall of cards.

Anatomy

A surface composed of optional regions.

  1. Container — the elevated/outlined surface (4px radius).
  2. Media — optional image/map thumbnail.
  3. Header — title, subtitle, optional avatar and overflow ⋮.
  4. Content — supporting text, KPIs, or a small table.
  5. Actions — a row of buttons (usually text/outlined).

Variants

VariantWhen to use
ElevatedDefault — a subtle shadow lifts the card off the page.
OutlinedFlatter surfaces / dense dashboards where shadows add noise.
With mediaWhen an image/map adds context (vehicle photo, location).
Actionable (whole-card)The entire card navigates to detail.

Behaviors

Actions

Keep card actions low-emphasis (text/outlined); reserve the contained button for the page's primary action.

Whole-card click

If the card is clickable, show hover/focus and don't bury separate interactive controls that compete with the card click.

Density

On dashboards, prefer compact cards in a grid; align heights for a tidy grid.

Do & Don't

Do

  • Keep one subject per card.
  • Use low-emphasis actions inside cards.
  • Align cards into a tidy grid on dashboards.

Don't

  • Don't replace a comparable-records table with many cards.
  • Don't stack multiple contained buttons in a card.
  • Don't nest cards within cards.

Accessibility

The card's title is a heading; an actionable card is a single link/button with an accessible name, or it exposes its inner controls clearly — not both in a confusing way. Focus is visible and order is logical.
